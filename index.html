WHAT IS THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN A CLUB AND A UNION?

A Club is a group of players and/or agents managed by one person – the Club’s creator.  Players within a Club can only play with one another unless the Club decides to create/join a Union.

A Union is a group of Clubs that share tables together.  Within a Union, all players from every Club in the Union can play against one another.

WHAT IS THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN A CLUB OWNER, MANAGER AND AGENT?

A Club owner can create a Union (under certain conditions), disband a Union (if they created it), disband the Club and promote members to the status of manager. 

A Club manager can:

- Promote a member to the status of agent

- Add/remove players to/from the Club

- Buy chips for the Club and send/claim back chips to/from players

- Grant/claim back credit

- Create/remove tables (unless they are part of a Union that has restricted the right to do so)

- Export data from the Club, with access to the data of all the players within the Club.

An agent is restricted to managing players directly under them.  Agents can send and claim back chips from players that are part of their own agent downline, track data during the week on players that are part of their downline and export data on players that are part of their downline.  

Agents cannot buy chips for downline players directly; they must request them from the Club.  Agents cannot add or remove players to/from the Club; they must request this from the Club.

WHAT IS A SUPER AGENT?

Super Agents are promoted agents with some of the permissions of Club managers – restricted to their own agents and downline players.  They can credit agents in their downline and send/claim chips to/from their downline agents and players, restrict access and grant VIP Cards, for example. A Club’s owner and managers can restrict some options for Super Agents.

Super Agents count towards the number of total managers as specified per Club level. So, for example, a Club with two managers and three Super Agents will need to be at Club Level 3 or higher.

HOW DO I ADD MORE CHIPS TO MY CLUB?

To add more chips, you must exchange Diamonds for chips within your Club.  To purchase Diamonds:

- Log into your account using your Player ID and password.

- In the upper right corner of the main lobby, there is a purple Diamond icon, along with a number displaying your current Diamond balance.  Click on the yellow “+” button.

- Choose one of the many available options that suits your needs best.

Once you have purchased Diamonds, enter your Club.  In the upper right corner of your Club lobby, there is a red chip icon, along with a number displaying your current Club chip balance.  Click on the yellow “+” button to exchange Diamonds for chips, and click “Confirm”. 

Note: Only the Club owner can exchange Diamonds for chips in a Club.  If you are a member of the Club, you have to request chips from your agent/Club owner.

HOW DO I TOP UP CHIPS WHILE AT A TABLE?

Click the menu icon in the top left of the screen and then “Top Up”.  Use the slider to choose how many chips you would like to add to your stack (your available total chips are visible beneath as “Account Balance”).  You can top up at any time, up to the maximum buy-in allowed on the table.  

WHAT DO THE ICONS NEXT TO THE AVATARS AND GAMES MEAN?

Broken Yellow Phone icon: A player has been offline for more than 30 days.

“C” icon: Players share the same Club.  For example, if there are two players showing a yellow “C” icon, and two players showing a green “C” icon, there are two players each at the table from two Clubs.

Gray Lock icon: The Agent/Manager has some restrictions.

Yellow Lock icon: A player is restricted from accessing some stakes.

Silver Key icon and Gold key icon: “Buy-in Authorization” and “Private Game”.  If both settings are ON, these will appear simultaneously. 

HOW DO “RUN IT MULTIPLE TIMES” AND “INSURANCE” WORK?

The “run it multiple times” and insurance options allow players to reduce variance.  Both of these features must be enabled by the table creator; you will know they are active when a small note appears in the center of the table in-game.  Both features are activated when players are all-in.  

For the “run it multiple times” feature, all-in players may opt to run out the remaining community cards once, twice or three times.  If the community cards run more than once, the pot will be split accordingly (for example, a player winning two out of three run-outs in the “run it three times” version will receive two thirds of the pot).

Insurance provides the favorite in an all-in situation a way to insure his (currently stronger) made hand against bad luck in the coming cards.  If the beat hits, a player who opted for insurance will be paid out according to what part of the pot they have insured. For more on insurance, including odds and Diamond cost to Clubs, click on the “?” icon next to “Insurance” in the table set-up screen.

HOW DOES THE BAD BEAT JACKPOT WORK?

Club owners can choose to enable the Bad Beat Jackpot (BBJ) feature in their Clubs by setting aside at least 3,000 Club Chips for it and then enabling the feature on individual tables.  When the Bad Beat Jackpot is enabled on a table, a small part of each pot larger than ten Big Blinds (post-flop) will be taken from the pot and contributed to the BBJ payout. Note: there must be at least four players dealt in preflop for the BBJ to be active.

The prize pool for the Bad Beat Jackpot is awarded when players hit certain Bad Beat hands. Click the Bad Beat Jackpot icon in the top right of a Club’s screen to see qualifying hands and payout information.

WHAT IS DISCONNECT PROTECTION AND HOW DOES IT WORK?

Disconnect protection is a feature designed to protect players if their network is unstable. Our system gives 60 seconds to the disconnected player to return to the table and will automatically check/fold during the protection time. After two hands, if the player still hasn’t reconnected, they will automatically sit out on all tables.

WHAT IS GPS/IP RESTRICTION?

Unlike competitors’ versions, our GPS restriction works by blocking players within 500 meters of each other from sitting at the same table.  IP restriction works by blocking accounts with the same IP address from sitting at the same table.  These act as preventative measures to stop collusion between players.

HOW DOES THE PHOTO VERIFICATION WORK?

When players join ring games or MTTs that have the feature enabled, a picture with a slider will pop up at random intervals during the game. Players then have 15 seconds to align the photo horizontally by dragging the slider.  

After three failed attempts (three successive photos, each with a maximum of three attempts within the 15 second time limit), ring game players will be removed and barred from joining the table again.  In MTTs, players won’t be removed from the game, but an alert will automatically be sent to Game Integrity BROS so that they can investigate.

To turn on Photo Rotating Verification, click the option in the menu after choosing “Create a new Table” (No Limit Hold’em, 6-Max Hold’em, Pot Limit Omaha or Open Face Chinese Poker). All players joining the table will then be subject to random spot checks using the photo/slider popup.

USE OF THIRD PARTY SOFTWARE

Third party software consists of using 3rd Party Tools  that manipulate gameplay. By altering game functionality, third party software aims to provide unfair advantages while putting your account and privacy at risk.


Third party software includes:

  1. Hacks, "mods", or programs that unfairly alter game functionality
  2. "Bots", or gameplay automation services or scripts
  3. Any other programs that aim to modify or provide users with information about other users actions not accessible to them without using such tools

Consequences of misconduct: Trying to gain an unfair advantage by using prohibited 3rd party software will result in a permanent ban for any offending account(s).
